Silver: Price, Control, and Trend Change

Technical structure vs narrative expectations

SLV Price Action (6-Month View)

iShares Silver Trust (SLV). Parabolic advance followed by structural failure.

Synthesis: Chart Structure vs Narrative

The SLV chart reflects a familiar pattern: a long period of suppression, followed by release, a parabolic blow-off, and then structural failure rather than consolidation. This behavior is characteristic of a completed move, not the early stages of a new secular bull market.

Kim Goguen’s framework predicts this exact sequence. In her view, silver pricing is administratively controlled: an artificial cap gives way to an artificial release, followed by an artificial spike and eventual reset. The process is discontinuous, not smoothly trending.

In practical terms, this creates a divergence between narrative and price action. Retail enthusiasm focuses on the story of silver “going to the moon,” while the chart reflects that the event has already been traded. The resulting breakdown aligns far more closely with a long-term trend change than with a temporary dip.

This Is More Than a Dip

The recent decline in silver is not behaving like a routine pullback within a healthy uptrend. The prior advance accelerated into a parabolic move, followed by sharp rejection, elevated volume on the selloff, and failure to reclaim the prior mean. These are classic characteristics of trend exhaustion and regime change, not simple consolidation.

Kim Goguen’s View on Silver

Kim consistently states that silver is not scarce and that its quoted price is administratively set rather than discovered. In her framework, silver’s importance is strategic and functional rather than monetary. As a result, price spikes often reflect control transitions rather than true value discovery.

Bottom Line

Kim does not view silver as a “moon asset,” nor does she expect linear price discovery to reward holders indefinitely. She describes silver as permanently valuable and functionally critical, but price-manipulated by design. In this context, the current chart structure supports a classic long-term break rather than the bullish retail consensus.
