Before You Begin - A Few Helpful Definitions:
---------------------------------------------
 
Epigenetics
-----------
Epigenetics is the study of how your environment and behaviors can affect how your genes work without changing the underlying DNA sequence.
 
Ontology (Faggin's meaning)
---------------------------
Ontology: what is fundamentally real in a system; the level where things truly exist. It answers the question, "What is the stuff of reality made of at the deepest level?" And in this interview, physics ontology = fields, not particles. Particles aren't "things." Particles are temporary ripples or excitations in an underlying field, and the field is the thing that actually exists.
 
When he says, "Ontology is in the field - it's not in the particles." he means: The field is the fundamental reality. The particle is just a transient state of that field. What you "see" as a particle is just a classical-scale effect of a tiny quantum-field event.
 
Why this matters in his argument: Because free will, choice, and consciousness (as he describes them) operate at the level of fields - not cells, not molecules, and not particles.
 
Seity
-----------
 
Seity is originally an English word meaning: selfhood; that which has identity; the fact of being oneself. Federico uses "seity" to mean a self-conscious quantum being.

Concepts and Theories:
---------------------
 
The concepts below are discussed or mentioned in this interview. None of them are particularly difficult - and understanding each of these at a conceptual level can make listening to this interview a more rewarding experience. These are basic principles underlying quantum mechanics.
 
-------------------------------
Einstein's Theory of Relativity (the important parts)
-------------------------------
 
1. Nothing can travel faster than light. This caps how fast information can move in our "classical" world - which is why quantum behavior looks so impossible.
 
2. Time is not absolute: observers moving relative to each other experience time differently. This fact affects the question of "what is information?" in a big way.
 
3. Space and time are one structure. Spacetime is a single unified backdrop, not two separate things. This matters because Faggin & D'Ariano argue that consciousness doesn't live inside spacetime - it creates or selects events in it.
 
4. Energy and mass are forms of information. Einstein's equation said simply that energy and matter are interchangeable. For Faggin, this is the stepping stone to: "Information is the fundamental layer beneath physics."
 
-----------------------------------
Three Quantum Rules You Can't Cheat
-----------------------------------
 
1. Holevo's Theorem: You can't extract all the information a quantum bit holds. Measurement collapses it, and only a tiny amount of classical information comes out.
 
2. No-Cloning Theorem: You can't copy an unknown quantum state. Trying to duplicate it destroys the very information you want to preserve.
 
3. No-Deleting Theorem: You can't erase a quantum state. If it exists, the information has to go somewhere - it can't just vanish.
 
-------------
Hilbert Space
-------------
 
Hilbert Space is a place of pure potential, where a quantum state exists before it becomes physical. It is more than just math - it's a real layer of being, where all possible states of a system live at once, and where choices are still open. It aligns well with the Causal Plane: both are where structure, intention, and possibility exist before collapsing into form, experience, or action.
 
----------------------
Schrodinger's Equation
----------------------
 
Schrodinger's equation tells you how a wavefunction changes - the wavefunction being the thing that contains all the possible states the quantum system could be in. But it doesn't tell you which outcome you'll get - it just tells you how all the possibilities evolve together until you look. It does this assuming standard conditions (without taking into account how time changes with speed - that is handled by Dirac's equation (below)).
 
So Schrodinger's equation is saying, "Here are all the possible futures - evolving smoothly, beautifully, mathematically - until someone observes the system. Then boom: one of them becomes the actual reality."
 
Schrodinger's Cat
-----------------
 
To show how weird this whole subject is, Schrodinger imagined a cat in a sealed box with a quantum trigger. If the quantum event happens, the cat dies. If it doesn't, the cat lives.
 
Quantum logic says: before you look, the system (represented by the cat) isn't "alive or dead." It's literally in a superposition: alive-and-dead at the same time, because both possibilities exist in the wavefunction. But as soon as you open the box, you force the system to choose one definite outcome - this is collapse.
 
The point isn't about the cat. The cat just emphasizes the definitive consequences of quantum reality, when applied to everyday objects.
 
-----------------
Dirac's Equation
-----------------
 
Dirac's equation describes how an electron behaves when it must obey both quantum rules (expressed by Schrodinger's equation) and Einstein's relativity. If Schrodinger's equation describes a slow electron, Dirac's equation describes a fast electron - one moving close to the speed of light.
 
And when Dirac combined those two realities, the math automatically predicted a new and shocking third reality: antimatter. Physicists saw this as a surprising new type of particle. But Dirac had unintentionally exposed something much bigger than a particle - his equation revealed the "mirror side" of creation; the Dark Universe.
