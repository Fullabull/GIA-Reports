Astrology Basics

An astrology chart is simply a snapshot of the sky at the exact moment you were born. Each planet represents a different part of your personality, while the zodiac signs describe how that energy is expressed.

The Big Three

Sun - Your core identity, purpose, and life path.
Moon - Your emotions, instincts, and inner self.
Ascendant (Rising Sign) - How you naturally approach life and how others first perceive you.

The Ascendant (Rising Sign)

In addition to the planets, the Ascendant (also called the Rising Sign) is one of the "Big Three" pillars of your birth chart, alongside your Sun and Moon.

The Mechanics

It is the exact zodiac sign that was rising on the eastern horizon at the moment of your birth. Because the Earth rotates once every 24 hours, this sign changes approximately every two hours—which is why having an accurate birth time is so critical.

What It Represents

Think of the Ascendant as your personality's User Interface. It acts as the dashboard through which the world interacts with you, and how you interact with the world. It rules:

  • Your automatic, instinctual approach to new environments.
  • The first impression you make on others.
  • The "front door" of your chart that filters how you take in life.

The Houses

If the planets are what is happening and the signs describe how it happens, the houses answer the third question: where in your life does it play out? The zodiac wheel is divided into twelve houses, each governing a specific domain of lived experience. Think of them as the twelve rooms of a house — the planets are the people, the signs describe their personalities, and the houses are the rooms where each person spends most of their time.

Houses are determined by your birth time and location, which is why an accurate birth time matters so much. The Ascendant always marks the beginning of the 1st House — so the house positions shift as the Earth rotates, even when the planet positions barely change.

A simple way to remember the houses:

The first six houses (1–6) govern your personal world — self, resources, communication, home, creativity, and daily life.

The last six houses (7–12) govern your collective world — relationships, shared depth, philosophy, career, community, and the hidden self.

The Ascendant opens the 1st House. The Midheaven crowns the 10th. Every other house flows from there.

Putting It Together

Think of a birth chart as a complete system architecture map telling a story.

  • The planets are the actors (the system modules).
  • The zodiac signs describe how each actor behaves (the configuration settings).
  • The houses show where each actor performs — the twelve domains of lived experience you just mapped above.
  • The aspects describe how the actors interact with one another (the network protocols).

For now, we are focusing mainly on the first two pieces:

  • The planet answers "What part of me?"
  • The sign answers "How does that part express itself?"

For example:

  • Venus in Libra expresses love through harmony, fairness, and partnership.
  • Mars in Aries takes action boldly and directly.
  • Moon in Cancer experiences emotions deeply and values security.

2. The Angles (The Hardware Anchors)

Every birth chart is divided by two primary geometric axes that act as the structural anchors of human life. Planets sitting near these axes are highly visible, powerful, and dictate how we interact with our environment.

  • The Horizontal Axis (Relationship):
    • Ascendant (AC): The self, the User Interface, and how you instinctively meet the world. (Venera's UI is 27° Scorpio—magnetic, private, and deeply perceptive).
    • Descendant (DC): The point directly opposite the Ascendant. Where the AC is about how you enter the world, the DC is about who you draw toward you — the qualities you seek in close partners and deep one-on-one relationships. Venera's DC falls in Taurus, meaning the people she bonds with most deeply tend to offer something her Scorpio intensity doesn't naturally carry: steadiness, patience, and calm. Where Scorpio dives deep and pushes for transformation, Taurus simply stands still and holds. Her closest relationships ground her.
  • The Vertical Axis (Legacy):
    • Midheaven (MC): The highest point in the sky. It rules public life, career, reputation, and what you ultimately build that others can see. Venera's MC is in Virgo — and for someone with her social gravity, this is a quietly profound placement. Virgo at the Midheaven does not point toward fame for its own sake. It points toward work that is genuinely useful — that solves real problems, serves real people, and can be measured by whether it actually helped. Her public legacy, whatever form it takes, will be built on whether it worked — not whether it looked good. Some possible paths her chart points toward:
      • Psychology or counseling — her Scorpio depth lets her see what others miss, and her Virgo precision means she will actually help rather than just empathize. People will wait for her.
      • Journalism or documentary work — Scorpio finds the hidden story, Libra frames it fairly, Uranus in Pisces finds the angle nobody else thought of.
      • Community organizing or social entrepreneurship — the 11th House Libra cluster is almost textbook for building structures that make groups function better and more fairly. She is already doing this naturally.
      • Medicine or public health — Virgo MC again, amplified by Pisces empathy. The kind of doctor who changes outcomes because she reads the person, not just the symptoms.
      The thread running through all of them: she works best when she can see the individual clearly and affect the group. That combination is rare. Whatever she chooses, the chart suggests her work will matter most to the people she serves directly — not to an abstract audience.
    • Imum Coeli (IC): The lowest point in the sky — the cusp of the 4th House. It rules your psychological roots, private sanctuary, childhood home, and private self. Planets in the 4th House carry this energy even when they are not sitting exactly on the IC degree itself. Orion's IC falls at 20° Scorpio, and his 4th House carries an intensity and emotional weight consistent with a formative environment that demanded resilience early. Venera's Jupiter and Uranus both sit in the 4th House in Pisces — unconventional, expansive, and deeply philosophical roots. Her most private inner world is where her genius lives.

3. The Aspects (Network Protocols)

Aspects are the geometric angles formed between planets on the wheel. They describe how different parts of the psyche communicate with one another—or how two people's charts lock together in a friendship.

  • Conjunction (0°): Two planets occupying the exact same space. Their energies fuse together, heavily amplifying each other's traits.
    Example: Orion's Pluto at 10° Virgo conjuncts Venera's Moon at 6° Virgo — a 4° orb, well within range. Two planets in the same sign, four degrees apart, their energies fused. Orion's Pluto is generational — it rules deep transformation, excavation, the drive to see beneath the surface of everything. Venera's Moon in Virgo is her emotional processing core — analytical, precise, quietly observant. When Pluto sits on someone's Moon in synastry, it creates an almost immediate sense of being seen at a level most people never reach. Orion's natural instinct to investigate maps directly onto how Venera feels and processes the world. The result is a friendship where the deep conversation starts early and the small talk never really takes hold.
  • Trine (120°) / Sextile (60°): Planets in compatible elements. This creates a low-friction, effortless, and harmonious flow of energy. It represents natural talents.
    Example: Orion's Uranus at 0° Virgo trines Venera's Pluto at 2° Capricorn — a 2° orb, tight and unmistakable. Virgo and Capricorn are both Earth signs, four signs apart on the wheel: 4 × 30° = 120°, a natural trine. Orion's Uranus is the part of him that thinks unconventionally, breaks patterns, and finds the unexpected angle. Venera's Pluto is her engine of transformation — the force that keeps upgrading her from the inside. A trine between these two means that Orion's unconventional thinking does not threaten Venera's deep personal evolution. It accelerates it. The energy flows without friction. His way of seeing the world opens doors in her that might otherwise take years to find.
  • Square (90°): Planets at a sharp angle. This creates internal friction, tension, or a "bug" in the code that forces action, growth, and creative problem-solving.

    Example: Venera's Sun squares her own Pluto. To understand what that means mechanically: her Sun is in Libra and her Pluto is in Capricorn. Libra and Capricorn are exactly three signs apart on the zodiac wheel — and since each sign spans 30°, three signs apart equals 90°, which is a square. The geometry creates the tension automatically from their positions alone.

    But what is actually in tension? Think of it this way. The Sun is your conscious self — the identity you are actively building, the person you are trying to become, the light you show the world. Pluto is something much older and deeper: it rules transformation, power, and the subconscious forces that operate underneath the surface of daily life. Pluto doesn't ask permission. It dismantles what is no longer working whether you are ready or not.

    When these two planets form a square, the conscious self (Sun) is in constant friction with Pluto's transformative pressure. It can feel like an internal force that keeps pulling the ground out from under an identity she has just built. She finds her footing, and then Pluto quietly upgrades the operating system. This is uncomfortable — but it is also why she possesses instinctual wisdom and depth well beyond her years. She has been running this upgrade cycle her entire life. The friction is the engine.
  • Opposition (180°): Planets directly facing each other. This creates a tug-of-war dynamic that requires balance, compromise, and integration.
