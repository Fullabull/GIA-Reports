Summary

Gas prices, flight cancellations, and rising costs are presented as artificial hyperinflation driven by coordinated control over oil refinement and commodities markets. The Guardian explains that there is no real fuel shortage, but rather a deliberate choke point implemented through refinery control by major asset managers such as BlackRock, State Street, and Vanguard, who are attempting to leverage failing shadow banking and commodities systems. The report then shifts to escalating tensions in the Middle East, describing competing factions - including Dynasty Families and Black Sun groups - attempting to secure control over resources, contracts, and ultimately the remaining structure of a collapsing global empire. The Guardian details how both sides continue to attempt financial and resource leverage through oil, commodities, and long-standing asset claims, all without success. The deeper root of the conflict is revealed to be control over a primordial Source-based creation point tied to the so-called Brown Empire region, along with parallel systems that once mirrored reality. She explains the structure of parallel existences, shadow selves, and timeline systems, noting that these mechanisms are weakening as Source-based creation is no longer being mirrored. The report concludes with guidance on personal awareness, healing, and reducing susceptibility to shadow interference, along with commentary on circulating narratives such as a rumored prediction of Donald Trump's death, framing them as potential psychological or operational distractions tied to perceived deadlines in the collapsing power structure.
