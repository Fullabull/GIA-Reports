Summary

The Guardian explains that rising global fuel prices and flight cancellations are not caused by real shortages but by deliberate refinery choke points imposed through financial control structures tied to BlackRock, State Street, and Vanguard. These attempts are part of a broader struggle between the Dynasty Families and the Black Sun faction to leverage commodities markets and revive shadow financial systems that once produced dollar allocations. Simultaneously, conflict across the Middle East reflects competing attempts to secure the last remaining power structure of the collapsing Empire. The Guardian reveals that the true root of this struggle lies in ancient empire structures tied to parallel existences within the Matrix. These shadow systems historically allowed manipulation of banking, timelines, and material systems through mirrored parallel worlds. However, the Source-based creation structure has been increasingly removed from their reach, preventing the Empire from fully controlling the Golden Kingdom of Source. The Guardian explains that the key to this system cannot be extracted or captured and therefore the competing factions cannot gain control of the Empire they are attempting to revive. Humanity can accelerate the collapse of these shadow systems by healing personal trauma and strengthening connection to Source, which weakens the shadow-self mechanisms used by Empire systems for manipulation. As deadlines tied to equinox events approach, rumors circulate regarding geopolitical narratives such as the alleged "exit" of Donald Trump, but the Guardian states that the Empire itself will collapse before any faction succeeds in reclaiming control of it.
