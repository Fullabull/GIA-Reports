Summary

Hans and Federico Faggin explore consciousness as the fundamental ontological reality, arguing that classical matter is only a representational interface of deeper quantum fields that possess identity, free will, and self-knowing. Faggin recounts a profound awakening experience at Lake Tahoe in 1990 in which he felt an expanded awareness and overwhelming love and white light, describing a state in which his consciousness existed both within and beyond the body while he was simultaneously the observer and the observed. This experience became pivotal in shaping his later work. He presents a new framework that introduces Quantum Information Panpsychism and the concept of the 'seity', describing beings as conscious quantum fields that arise when One, the fundamental consciousness, knows itself through infinitely many perspectives. Ontology, he argues, resides in the quantum fields themselves, not in particles, which are merely classical echoes of transient field events. In his broader framework, these fields reside in the domain physics formalizes as Hilbert space - a space of all possible states - which closely parallels what metaphysical traditions call the Causal Plane, the blueprint realm where potential meaning and intention precede physical manifestation. The discussion spans near-death experiences, the limitations of classical AI, the quantum foundations of life, the role of cooperation over competition, and how shared resonance between conscious beings can generate higher-level seities through unified internal states.
